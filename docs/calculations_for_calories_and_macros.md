## Calculations for Calories and Macros

### 1. Calculate Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)
The BMR is the number of calories your body burns at rest.

**Mifflin-St Jeor Equation (Most Accurate)**

**Men:**
BMR=(10×weight in kg)+(6.25×height in cm)−(5×age in years)+5

**Women:**
BMR=(10×weight in kg)+(6.25×height in cm)−(5×age in years)−161

### 2. Adjust for Activity Level (TDEE)
Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) accounts for activity level by multiplying BMR by an activity factor:

| Activity Level                     | Multiplier |
|------------------------------------|------------|
| Sedentary (little or no exercise)  | 1.2        |
| Lightly active (1-3 days/week)     | 1.375      |
| Moderately active (3-5 days/week)  | 1.55       |
| Very active (6-7 days/week)        | 1.725      |
| Super active (hard exercise daily) | 1.9        |

TDEE=BMR×Activity Factor

### 3. Adjust Calories for Goal
Once we know TDEE, we adjust for the fitness goal:

| Goal               | Caloric Adjustment                      |
|--------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Cutting (Fat Loss) | TDEE - 500 kcal (Moderate Deficit)      |
| Maintenance        | TDEE (No Adjustment)                    |
| Bulking (Muscle Gain) | TDEE + 250-500 kcal (Mild Surplus)   |

For extreme fat loss, a 750-1000 kcal deficit can be used, but it's not always sustainable. For aggressive bulking, a 500-1000 kcal surplus can be used.

Daily Calories=TDEE±Goal Adjustment

### 4. Macronutrient Breakdown
Macronutrients are typically split as a percentage of total calories, based on the goal.

**General Macro Ratios**

| Goal       | Protein | Carbs  | Fats   |
|------------|---------|--------|--------|
| Cutting    | 40%     | 30-40% | 20-30% |
| Maintenance| 30%     | 40-50% | 20-30% |
| Bulking    | 25-35%  | 45-55% | 15-25% |

To calculate macros:

**Protein Calculation**
Protein is set at 1.6–2.2 g per kg of body weight (higher for cutting).

Protein (grams)= (Calories×Protein %)÷4
​
(Since 1g of protein = 4 kcal)

**Fat Calculation**
Fats are 15-30% of total calories (higher for maintenance).

Fats (grams)= (Calories×Fat %)÷9

(Since 1g of fat = 9 kcal)

**Carb Calculation**
Carbs make up the remaining calories.

Carbs (grams)=(Total Calories−(Protein kcal+Fat kcal))÷4

### 5. Example Calculation
For a 25-year-old male, 180 cm, 80 kg, moderately active, bulking (+300 kcal surplus):

**BMR Calculation:**
\[ \text{BMR} = (10 \times 80) + (6.25 \times 180) - (5 \times 25) + 5 \]
\[ \text{BMR} = 800 + 1125 - 125 + 5 = 1805 \text{ kcal} \]

**TDEE Calculation:**
\[ \text{TDEE} = 1805 \times 1.55 = 2798 \text{ kcal} \]

**Bulking Calories:**
\[ 2798 + 300 = 3098 \text{ kcal} \]

**Macronutrient Breakdown:**

**Protein:** 30% of 3098 kcal
3098×0.30=929.4 kcal÷4=232.4 g

**Fats:** 20% of 3098 kcal
3098×0.20=619.6 kcal÷9=68.8 g

**Carbs:** Remaining calories (3098 - (929.4 + 619.6))
1549 kcal÷4=387.3 g

**Final Plan:**

- Calories: 3098 kcal
- Protein: 232g
- Fats: 69g
- Carbs: 387g
